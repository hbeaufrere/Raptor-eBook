# California

In addition to federal permits, anyone possessing or rehabilitating raptors in California must comply with state regulations administered by the California Department of Fish and Wildlife (CDFW). California law imposes its own permitting requirements, species restrictions, and facility standards that operate alongside and in addition to federal rules. The following is an overview of the key California regulations relevant to raptor rehabilitation, education, and captive management.

### CDFW Title 14 CCR 679 – Possession of Wildlife and Wildlife Rehabilitation

_California Fish and Game Code, Title 14, California Code of Regulations_

Title 14 CCR Section 679 is the primary California regulation governing wildlife rehabilitation. It establishes the state permitting framework for individuals and facilities that take in injured, sick, or orphaned native wildlife, including raptors. Under this regulation, the CDFW issues wildlife rehabilitation permits that authorize the temporary possession of native wildlife for the purpose of treatment, conditioning, and release. The regulation specifies minimum standards for facilities, veterinary oversight, record-keeping, and reporting. Rehabilitators must submit annual reports detailing the number and disposition of all animals received. Section 679 also outlines the conditions under which non-releasable animals may be retained, transferred to educational facilities, or euthanized. California rehabilitation permits are required in addition to the federal USFWS rehabilitation permit, and both must be held concurrently to legally rehabilitate raptors in the state.

Official resource: https://www.wildlife.ca.gov/Licensing/Wildlife-Rehabilitation

### CDFW Title 14 CCR 671 – Restricted Species

_California Fish and Game Code, Title 14, California Code of Regulations_

Section 671 of Title 14 CCR maintains California's list of restricted species — animals that may not be imported, transported, possessed, or released in the state without a special permit. This regulation is particularly relevant to raptor keeping because it governs which raptor species may be legally possessed in California and under what conditions. Non-native raptor species, as well as certain native species with special conservation status, may be classified as restricted. Falconers, rehabilitators, and educators must ensure that any raptor species they possess is not listed as restricted, or that they hold the appropriate exemption or permit. Section 671 is also important in the context of preventing the introduction of non-native species that could compete with or prey upon native wildlife. Violations of restricted species regulations can result in confiscation of animals and significant penalties.

Official resource: https://www.wildlife.ca.gov/Conservation/Invasive-Species/Regulations/Restricted-Species

### CDFW Title 14 CCR 650 – Scientific, Educational Purpose

_California Fish and Game Code, Title 14, California Code of Regulations_

Section 650 of Title 14 CCR authorizes the CDFW to issue Scientific Collecting Permits (SCPs) and educational permits for the possession and use of wildlife, including raptors, for bona fide scientific research and educational purposes. This regulation is relevant to universities, museums, research institutions, and educational organizations that maintain live raptors or raptor specimens for teaching, research, or public outreach. Under Section 650, permit holders may collect, possess, and use wildlife in ways that would otherwise be prohibited, provided the activity serves a legitimate scientific or educational objective. The permit application requires a detailed description of the proposed activities, species involved, and the qualifications of the applicant. In the context of raptor rehabilitation and education, Section 650 provides the state-level authorization that complements the federal USFWS education permit, and both permits must be held to legally use raptors for educational programming in California.

Official resource: https://www.wildlife.ca.gov/Licensing/Scientific-Collecting
