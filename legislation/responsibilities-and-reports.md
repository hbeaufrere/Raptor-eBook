# Responsibilities and Reports

Operating a raptor rehabilitation, education, or falconry program requires maintaining multiple permits at both the federal and state levels, each with its own renewal schedules, reporting obligations, and record-keeping requirements. Failure to submit required reports or allow scheduled inspections can result in permit suspension or revocation. The following is a summary of the key permits that must be maintained, the reports that must be filed, and the ongoing responsibilities associated with each regulatory framework described in this section.



### Permits to Maintain

Depending on the scope of the operation, the following permits may need to be obtained and kept current. Each permit has specific renewal intervals, and most require annual or biennial reporting as a condition of renewal.



USFWS Federal Migratory Bird Rehabilitation Permit (50 CFR 21.76): Required for any individual or facility that takes in injured, sick, or orphaned migratory birds, including raptors, for the purpose of rehabilitation and release. Must be renewed every 5 years through the USFWS online permit system (ePERMITS).



USFWS Special Purpose Possession Permit for Education (50 CFR 21.95): Required for the possession and use of live or dead migratory birds and their parts for educational purposes. Covers non-releasable education birds held for public programming. Must be renewed every 5 years.



USFWS Eagle Exhibition Permit (50 CFR 22.50): Required for facilities that possess bald or golden eagles for educational exhibition. Subject to specific housing and display requirements under the Bald and Golden Eagle Protection Act. Must be renewed every 5 years.



USFWS Federal Falconry Permit (50 CFR 21.82): Required for the practice of falconry, including the capture and possession of raptors for falconry purposes. Permit classes (Apprentice, General, Master) have different bird possession limits. Administered jointly with state agencies and must be renewed every 5 years.



USDA Animal Welfare Act Exhibitor License: Required for facilities that exhibit raptors to the public, including educational programs that charge admission or receive compensation. Subject to USDA APHIS inspections. Must be renewed annually.



USGS Federal Bird Banding Permit (50 CFR Parts 10, 13, 21): Required for the capture and banding of wild birds for research, monitoring, or rehabilitation tracking. Issued by the USGS Bird Banding Laboratory. Must be renewed every 3 years.



CDFW California Wildlife Rehabilitation Permit (Title 14 CCR 679): Required for any individual or facility rehabilitating native wildlife in California. Must be held concurrently with the federal USFWS rehabilitation permit. Renewal is annual, and the permit is contingent on submission of required reports.



CDFW Scientific, Educational, or Propagation Permit (Title 14 CCR 650): Required for possession of wildlife for scientific collecting, educational use, or propagation in California. Must be renewed annually.



UC Davis IACUC Protocol Approval: Required for any use of live vertebrate animals in teaching, research, or clinical activities at UC Davis. Protocols must be submitted for initial review and are subject to annual continuing review, with full re-review every 3 years.



### Required Reports and Filings

The following reports and filings are required under federal and California regulations. Deadlines are strict, and late or missing reports can jeopardize permit renewal.



USFWS Annual Report (Rehabilitation Permit): All holders of USFWS migratory bird rehabilitation permits (50 CFR 21.76) must submit an annual report detailing the number and species of birds received, the disposition of each bird (released, died, transferred, euthanized), and any other information required by the Regional Director. Reports are submitted through the USFWS ePERMITS system by January 31 of each year for the preceding calendar year.



USFWS Annual Report (Education Permit): Holders of special purpose possession permits for education (50 CFR 21.95) must file annual reports documenting all education birds in their possession, any acquisitions, transfers, or deaths during the reporting period, and the educational programs conducted.



USFWS Eagle Permit Reports (50 CFR 22.50): Eagle permit holders must report on the status, health, and disposition of all eagles in their care. Any eagle death must be reported within 5 business days, and the carcass must be shipped to the National Eagle Repository.



USFWS Falconry Reports (50 CFR 21.82): Falconers must report the capture, acquisition, transfer, loss, or death of any raptor within 10 business days through the state or federal reporting system. Annual inventory reports may also be required depending on state regulations.



USGS Bird Banding Data Submissions: All banding data must be reported to the USGS Bird Banding Laboratory through the Bandit online system. Data submissions include species, band number, date, location, age, sex, and condition at banding. Encounter reports (recaptures, recoveries) must also be reported promptly.



USDA Annual Report of Research Facility: Facilities holding USDA exhibitor licenses or research registrations must submit annual reports to APHIS detailing the number and species of animals held, any painful or distressful procedures, and the use of anesthetics or analgesics. Reports are due by December 1 each year.



CDFW Annual Rehabilitation Report (Title 14 CCR 679): California wildlife rehabilitators must submit an annual report to CDFW detailing the number, species, and disposition of all wildlife received during the year. The report is typically due by March 31 following the reporting year.



CDFW Scientific Collecting Permit Annual Report (Title 14 CCR 650): Holders of scientific collecting permits must submit annual reports describing the species collected, quantities, methods, and disposition. Failure to report results in automatic permit suspension.



CITES Annual Trade Reports (50 CFR Part 23): If any raptors or raptor specimens are imported or exported, CITES reporting requirements apply. The USFWS Division of Management Authority compiles the U.S. annual report to the CITES Secretariat, and permit holders must maintain accurate records of all international transactions.



UC Davis IACUC Annual Continuing Review: All active IACUC protocols must undergo annual continuing review. The principal investigator must submit a progress report confirming ongoing activities, any adverse events, and compliance with approved procedures. Failure to complete annual review results in protocol suspension.



### Record-Keeping and Inspections

In addition to filing formal reports, permit holders must maintain accurate, contemporaneous records that are available for inspection at any time. USFWS rehabilitation permits require a log of every bird admitted, including species, date of admission, origin, diagnosis, treatment provided, and final disposition. USDA-licensed facilities must maintain records for at least three years and make them available to APHIS inspectors during unannounced site visits. CDFW may conduct facility inspections as a condition of state rehabilitation permits, and falconry permits are subject to raptor housing inspections by state wildlife officers. IACUC-approved activities are subject to semiannual facility inspections and ongoing post-approval monitoring by the UC Davis Animal Care Program. All records should be organized and readily accessible, as failure to produce records during an inspection can be treated as a permit violation.
